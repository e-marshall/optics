2. equation for net radiation (incoming - outgoing)#

Net radiation equations for each square

\[ E_{net} = SW_{incoming} + LW_{incoming} - SW_{outgoing} \]

Black square:

\[ E_{net} = 0.95E_{o}cos\theta+{\varepsilon}_{square}{\sigma}T^4(0.95) - {\varepsilon}_{square}{\sigma}T^4 \]

White square:

\[ E_{net} = 0.05E_{o}cos\theta+{\varepsilon}_{square}{\sigma}T^4(0.05) - {\varepsilon}_{square}{\sigma}T^4 \]

3. Would the squares ever reach the same temperature in a radiatively stable environment?#

No, because of the difference in albedo of the two squares, the black square will always absorb more radiation than the white square and the squares will never reach the same temperature.

The change in the cooling rater over time of the foil-covered container is less than that of the glass container. They will eventually reach the same equilibrium temperature but it will take different times for each to do so.
